Hi everyone,

As we all know smeagol does the contour integration of G*F above the real axis in order to bypass the singularities. If the definition of G is 

G = inv(ES-H), it's no doubt. But my question is: right now we introduce self-energy due to the existence of leads, and self-energy is also a function of energy, so is it still true that all the singularities of G*F are located only on real axis and imag axis at fermi level? Isn't there other poles on the positive complex plane?

I did a little computation which shows that if the overlap between basis sets of scattering region and leads is non-zero, (with bounds of energy and fermi level fixed, of course), the integral depends on the number of poles adopted at the fermi level. If there's no overlap, the integral is independent of the number of poles. I think this demostrates addtional poles other than ones at fermi level due to the overlap between scattering region and leads.

By the way, the shape of contour i used is rectangular, instead of semi-circle in the manual. Do you think that matters?

Many thanks!

Bertrand

Dear Reza,

The leads calculation should be periodic and include k-points along the z direction (1 x 1 x n, if you haven't got k-points along x and y)to simulate an infinite gold electrode. If you have Au(111) you should have the following layers in the bulk and transport calculations:

Bulk: ABC
Transport: ABCAB-CNT-BCABC

The first and last layers of the transport calculation should be bulk layers. Of course you can include more layers in the middle to converge the calculation if the screening in the electrodes is poor.

The electrodes size along x and y should be at least big enough to avoid overlaps between nanotubes along these directions, which you can calculate taking into account the longest radii of the basis set.

Victor
